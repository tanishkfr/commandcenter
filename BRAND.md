# Remainder

**What matters, remains.**

Remainder is a project-memory workspace where conversations become durable creative context. It is not a dashboard, an archive of everything, or an AI persona. It is the quiet layer that lets a project remember how it became itself.

> Naming note: this is a product-direction decision, not legal clearance. A preliminary product scan removed the clearest direct collisions; trademark and domain review should happen before a commercial launch.

### Why Remainder

Remainder turns the product's behavior into a memorable idea. A conversation is temporary; its useful consequence should not be. The word is familiar but unexpected in software, works across design, research, writing, and making, and gives the interface a rigorous editing principle: keep what changes the work. It creates a complete verbal world without jargon—what remains, what carries forward, what is worth keeping.

Pentimento, Palimpsest, Threadmark, Patina, and Afterword were set aside after finding active products in adjacent creative-memory or workflow territory. Remainder showed no comparable direct category collision in the preliminary scan.

## Strategy

### Mission

Help creative work remember the conversations, decisions, questions, and changes that gave it form.

### Vision

Every project carries an intelligible history—not a pile of transcripts, but a living record that helps the next good decision happen.

### Core thesis

Creative work loses intelligence when its reasoning disappears. Conversation becomes valuable when judgment turns it into durable, traceable context.

### Personality

Quiet, exact, warm, observant, and assured. Remainder has taste without theatricality. It never performs intelligence; it makes the work more intelligible.

### Voice

Editorial, plainspoken, and gently directive. Prefer precise verbs and complete thoughts. Speak about the work, not the software. Never use hype, faux intimacy, magic language, or claims that the product “knows everything.”

### Values

1. **Continuity over accumulation.** Preserve the line of thought, not every token.
2. **Judgment over automation.** The person decides what enters memory.
3. **Provenance over certainty.** Every durable thought can be traced to its source.
4. **Calm over velocity theatre.** Fast should feel immediate, not frantic.
5. **Craft over novelty.** Every detail should earn its place.
6. **Ownership over lock-in.** Memory stays private, portable, and recoverable.

### Positioning

**Remainder is a project-memory workspace that turns conversations into reviewed, traceable creative context.**

## Visual identity

### The mark: The Carry

Two interlocking right-angle forms make the mark. The ink form is the present conversation. The oxide form begins before the first has fully ended and carries forward: memory as continuity, not storage. Their overlap is intentional—the meaning is made in the handoff.

The mark is abstract and readable at 16 px. It contains no brain, bubble, sparkle, robot, or letterform gimmick. Never rotate it, outline it, add shadows, or recolor its two pieces independently outside approved monochrome use.

### Wordmark and icon

Set **Remainder** in Instrument Sans Medium with optical kerning and -0.035em tracking. Use title case; never abbreviate the name.

The app icon is a warm-ground square with a 22% corner radius containing The Carry. The container is a platform convention, not part of the core mark.

### Color

| Token | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Ink | `#25231E` | Primary type, anchors, decisive actions |
| Ground | `#F1EEE7` | Navigation and surrounding environment |
| Paper | `#FAF8F2` | Working surface |
| Soft paper | `#F6F3EC` | Layered context surfaces |
| Rule | `rgba(37,35,30,.13)` | Structure without boxes |
| Ash | `#746E64` | Supporting copy |
| Oxide | `#A44932` | Memory, focus, and the act of keeping |
| Oxide wash | `#F0DED7` | Selected and newly retained states |
| Pine | `#3F665A` | Confirmed and healthy states |
| Carmine | `#9C3F37` | Destructive or failed states only |

Oxide comes from proof marks, fired clay, and pigment that survives handling—human evidence rather than digital spectacle. It is warm enough for creative work and disciplined enough for a serious tool. Purple is removed because its software associations overstate “AI magic” and weaken the thesis.

### Typography

- **Instrument Sans** — interface, navigation, controls, and labels.
- **Source Serif 4** — thoughts, headings, empty states, quotations, and narrative.
- **DM Mono** — timestamps, shortcuts, provenance, and technical values only.

Serif marks reflective content; sans marks action. Never use the serif decoratively on buttons.

### Spacing

A 4 px rhythm: `4 · 8 · 12 · 16 · 24 · 32 · 48 · 64 · 96`.

Dense controls use 8–12 px internal spacing. Reading surfaces use 24–48 px. Major sections earn 64–96 px of air. Alignment matters more than symmetry.

### Radius

- 4 px: indicators
- 6 px: controls
- 10 px: cards and menus
- 16 px: dialogs and onboarding
- 999 px: status pills only

Avoid inflated cards. Remainder should feel bound and composed, not bubbly.

### Surfaces and illustration

Use paper-like tonal shifts, fine rules, and occasional inset edges. Most grouping comes from spacing and typography. Shadows appear only when a surface genuinely moves above another.

Illustration uses editorial diagrams: cropped working notes, construction lines, linked fragments, translucent layers, and annotated sequences. Never use mascots, pseudo-3D objects, smiling characters, glowing orbs, or generated “AI” scenery.

## Motion

Remainder moves in three verbs:

1. **Reveal** — context enters at 140–180 ms with opacity and 4–6 px of travel.
2. **Carry** — a kept thought moves toward memory over 220–280 ms; the destination responds first.
3. **Settle** — capture and project changes resolve over 320–420 ms with restrained ease-out.

Use `cubic-bezier(.22,.8,.24,1)`. Avoid bounce, elastic scale, confetti, and perpetual ambient motion.

When a conversation is kept, candidates appear in source order. A kept item shifts 8 px toward memory and changes from paper to oxide wash. The count updates at the end; undo remains available. Context changes crossfade. Project switching keeps the frame stable. Reduced motion removes travel and uses opacity only.

The Carry never spins. Its oxide corner advances once, then settles. Skeletons preserve final geometry to prevent layout shift.

### Microinteractions

- Hover raises contrast, not scale.
- Press moves 1 px with no spring.
- Focus uses a 2 px oxide ring and 3 px offset.
- New memory receives one brief oxide wash.
- Destructive actions remain neutral until confirmation.
- Tooltips appear only where a label cannot fit.

## Principles

1. The project is the object; AI is infrastructure.
2. Keep less, but make what is kept trustworthy.
3. Show where every memory came from.
4. Preserve the user's place through every state change.
5. Let motion explain continuity.
6. Write for a thoughtful person, not a software operator.
7. If a surface cannot reach a meaningful conclusion, remove it.
