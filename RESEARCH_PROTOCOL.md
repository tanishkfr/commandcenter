# Remainder research protocol

## Purpose

Evaluate whether reviewed, traceable project memory helps creative practitioners resume work and understand changed direction better than transcript-only recall. This is a protocol, not a claim that research already happened.

## Participants

Recruit 8–12 product designers, design researchers, writers, creative technologists, or graduate design students who conduct multi-session work. Include regular and infrequent conversational-AI users. Do not recruit only people who already understand the concept.

## Study

### Session 1 — 45 minutes

Obtain consent. Observe onboarding without coaching. Begin or import a real project. Capture and review candidates. Introduce a related new direction. Ask the participant to choose between keeping alongside and changing direction, then interview their mental model.

### Diary — 7 days

Use one real project in at least three sessions. After each, record expectations, corrections, dismissals, supersessions, hesitation, distrust, and time needed to resume.

### Session 2 — 45 minutes

After at least 48 hours away, locate a decision and rationale, explain changed direction from lineage, undo or revise memory, compare against existing notes, and identify overconfident claims.

## Measures

Observe completion without help, time to find rationale, time to resume work, incorrect acceptance, correction and undo success, first-query search success, and confusion between alongside and change direction. Then collect 7-point confidence, interruption-cost, and future-use ratings with explanations.

## Analysis and thresholds

Tag breakdowns by comprehension, trust, recovery, interruption, and value. Preserve negative cases. If more than 2 of 10 participants mistake supersession for deletion, redesign lineage. If fewer than 70% recover rationale unaided, redesign provenance. If review cost outweighs value, reduce extraction volume. If inaccurate candidates are routinely accepted, favor manual capture.
